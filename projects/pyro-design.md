# Process Design: 4,000 TPD Pyro-Processing Unit
**Engineering Design Project** | *System Sizing & Induced Draft Optimization*

---

## 🏗️ Design Objective
The goal of this project was the complete process design and sizing of a pyro-processing string for a greenfield cement plant with a capacity of **4,000 TPD**. This involved calculating the mass and energy requirements to determine the physical dimensions and power ratings of critical equipment.

## 📐 Key Design Components & Calculations

### 1. Pre-Heater Cyclone Sizing
Designed a 5-stage cyclone string to maximize heat transfer between exhaust gases and raw meal. Sizing was based on achieving optimal separation efficiency while minimizing pressure drop.

### 2. In-Line Pre-Calciner (ILC)
Sized the calciner volume to ensure a **90-95% calcination degree** before the meal enters the kiln.
* **Basis:** Residence time, CO2 partial pressure, and fuel combustion kinetics.

### 3. Induced Draft (ID) Fan Specifications
Calculated the total system resistance (Pressure Drop) to specify the ID Fan power requirements.

$$\text{Power (kW)} = \frac{Q \cdot \Delta P}{1000 \cdot \eta_{fan}}$$

Where:
* $Q$ = Volumetric flow rate ($m^3/s$)
* $\Delta P$ = Total system pressure drop ($Pa$)
* $\eta_{fan}$ = Fan efficiency
